Comprehensive Analysis of Financial Inclusion in Rwanda

This data analytics project on financial inclusion in Rwanda reveals that, despite progress in enhancing access to financial services, barriers persist for certain population segments, particularly in rural areas and among lower-income households. Recommendations for improvement include expanding bank branches in underserved areas, advocating for mobile money usage, and implementing financial education programs. Executed using R, with additional visualizations created using Microsoft Power BI, the project underscores the ongoing need for efforts to enhance financial inclusion, ensuring equal economic participation for all individuals and businesses. Comparative Analysis Between Multiple Regression and Time Series Approaches to Factors Influencing IMR

In this project, I conducted a comprehensive analysis of infant mortality rates (IMR) in Kenya, utilizing both regression and time series approaches. The multiple regression analysis identified significant predictors. The wealth index emerged as the strongest predictor, indicating a 5.6% decrease in IMR with each additional wealth index. Additionally, time series analysis revealed a declining trend in IMRs over time, along with seasonal variations. The findings suggest that targeted interventions, such as promoting education, economic empowerment, and region-specific healthcare strategies, can contribute to reducing IMRs in Kenya. The forecast from the ARIMA(1,1,1) model provides insights for the next 20 years, offering a holistic understanding of IMR determinants and trends for informed policy-making.
